Infera provides probabilistic interpretations of publicly available signals. It is informational only and is not financial, betting, legal, medical, or professional advice.

How it works

Infera ingests public feeds — news, sports, social, and markets — scores them for anomaly and salience, and projects each active event onto a library of historical patterns to produce calibrated probability estimates with confidence levels.

Ingest

Curated public data streams are polled and cached so the inference layer always has a fresh view without hammering upstream APIs.

Score

Each source is normalized and combined into a single event score. Nothing is surfaced until that score clears a calibrated threshold.

Project

Active events are projected onto historical analogues to produce outcome probabilities with a confidence label.

About

Infera is an independent research project. The live endpoint above is a free-tier deployment — first requests after an idle period can take up to a minute to wake. The widget retries automatically.
